## Purpose

This document tracks possible combat directions. No combat system has been selected yet.

## Current Status

Status: Not implemented.

Combat is intentionally undefined in the current PoC. Existing stats provide a starting vocabulary, but they do not imply a finalized battle design.

## Possible Directions

### Turn Based

Status: Exploration.

Pros:

- Easy to read.
- Works well with collection and stats.
- Good fit for abilities and status effects.

Cons:

- Can feel familiar unless the photo-generated identity matters.
- Requires careful pacing.

### Real Time

Status: Exploration.

Pros:

- More immediate and kinetic.
- Could support animated or spatial creatures.

Cons:

- Harder to balance.
- Requires more UI, animation, and input work.

### Card Based

Status: Exploration.

Pros:

- Strong fit for short sessions.
- Allows simple stats to become strategic.
- Easy to expand through abilities.

Cons:

- May shift attention away from the creature itself.
- Requires deck, hand, draw, and resource rules.

### Rhythm Based

Status: Exploration.

Pros:

- Distinctive and expressive.
- Could connect to creature personality.

Cons:

- High implementation and accessibility cost.
- May not fit all players.

### Grid

Status: Exploration.

Pros:

- Makes positioning meaningful.
- Supports range, terrain, and team tactics.

Cons:

- Increases complexity quickly.
- Requires strong UI clarity on small screens.

### Deck Builder

Status: Exploration.

Pros:

- Deep replayability.
- Good support for synergies and procedural variety.

Cons:

- Could overwhelm the collection fantasy.
- Needs large content volume.

## Evaluation Criteria

Future prototypes should be judged by:

- Does the creature origin matter?
- Can a player understand why they won or lost?
- Does combat encourage collecting different creatures?
- Can battles stay short?
- Can the system scale without excessive content burden?

## Open Questions

- Is combat one creature versus one creature, team based, or collection based?
- Do roles map directly to battle actions?
- Do materials become elements, resistances, or ability tags?
- How much randomness is acceptable?
- Should battles happen locally, online, asynchronously, or all of these?
